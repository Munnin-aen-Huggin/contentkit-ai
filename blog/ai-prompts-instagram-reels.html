10 AI Prompts for Instagram Reels Scripts That Get Views

ContentKit AI•12 min read

AI prompts for Instagram Reels are structured instructions that generate scroll-stopping hook scripts, educational tutorials, product demos, behind-the-scenes content, and 30-day content calendars — scripted to the second and optimized for watch time, shares, and the Reels algorithm. This guide delivers 10 copy-paste prompts covering every major Reels format, each built on the hook-body-CTA framework that high-performing creators use to consistently reach new audiences.

Instagram Reels is the highest-reach format on the platform right now. Meta has publicly confirmed that Reels gets prioritized distribution to non-followers, which means a well-scripted Reel can reach tens of thousands of new people who have never heard of your brand — without spending a dollar on ads. That is a distribution opportunity that no other format on Instagram currently offers at the same scale.

But here is what most creators and brands get wrong: they treat Reels like a shorter version of their existing content. They take a blog post, read the bullet points to camera, and wonder why no one watches past the five-second mark. Reels is not a short version of long-form content. It is a completely different format with its own rules, rhythms, and audience expectations. The hook must land in the first three seconds or the viewer swipes. The pacing must be tight enough to hold attention but slow enough to be understood. The CTA must feel natural, not bolted on at the end.

This is where structured ai prompts for instagram reels change the equation. Instead of staring at a blank script document, you paste a prompt into ChatGPT or Claude, fill in a few variables about your niche, topic, and audience, and get a complete script — hook, body, visuals, text overlays, and CTA — in under two minutes. The AI handles the structure. You handle the performance.

The 10 prompts below cover every major Reels format: hook-only scripts for the critical first three seconds, full educational tutorials, before-and-after transformation stories, day-in-the-life narratives, product demos and unboxings, trending audio adapters, behind-the-scenes content, customer testimonials, carousel-to-Reel repurposing, and a complete 30-day content calendar. Why AI Prompts Produce Better Reels Scripts

The Instagram algorithm rewards one thing above all others: watch time. If viewers watch your Reel all the way through — or better yet, rewatch it — the algorithm interprets that as a signal that your content is valuable and distributes it to more people. Everything about how you script a Reel should be engineered to hold attention from the first frame to the last second.

Here is why structured AI prompts consistently produce scripts that hold attention better than scripts written from scratch.

Structure Forces the Hook First

The single biggest mistake in Reels scripting is burying the value. Creators say "today I am going to show you how to..." before actually showing anything interesting. Viewers are gone by the time the value arrives. AI prompts that specify "write the hook in the first line, make it a bold claim or pattern interrupt, and do not explain context before the hook" force the output to lead with the most compelling element. The viewer sees the hook, decides to stay, and then gets the context. Not the other way around.

Frameworks Eliminate the Blank Page

Every high-performing Reel follows a pattern, even when it looks spontaneous. Educational Reels follow: hook, numbered steps, quick recap, CTA. Transformation Reels follow: before state, turning point, after state, how to get there. Day-in-the-life Reels follow: morning anchor, 3-4 scene cuts, honest reflection, call to follow. These patterns exist because they match how human attention works — we need a reason to watch, a clear structure to follow, and a satisfying endpoint. AI prompts encode these patterns so every script has a framework underneath it, even if the viewer never notices.

Pacing and Visual Direction Are Built In

A script without visual direction is just words. The prompts in this article instruct the AI to include shot descriptions, text overlay suggestions, transition cues, and on-screen visual prompts alongside the spoken script. That means you get a complete production brief — what to say, what to show, and when to cut — not just a block of text to read to camera.

The bottom line: ai prompts for instagram reels do not replace your creativity or your unique perspective. They give every script a professional structure, an attention-engineered hook, and a clear visual plan so you can record faster and publish more consistently. Here are the 10 prompts.

10 AI Prompts for Instagram Reels

Prompt 1: Reels Hook Script (First 3 Seconds)

The hook is the only part of your Reel that matters if nobody watches the rest. Instagram's feed is a scroll-and-decide environment: viewers give you roughly three seconds to earn their attention before they swipe to the next video. That three-second window must contain something that creates curiosity, triggers recognition, or makes a bold enough claim that the viewer cannot leave without knowing more. This prompt generates five different hook variations for the same topic — each using a different psychological framework — so you can test which style resonates most with your audience.

Prompt IR-01: Reels Hook Script (First 3 Seconds)
Act as an Instagram Reels strategist who has studied thousands of viral short-form videos. Write 5 hook variations for the first 3 seconds of a Reel on the topic below. Each hook must stop the scroll immediately.

Hook frameworks to use (one per variation):
— Bold Claim: State something counterintuitive or surprising about the topic
— Direct Callout: Speak directly to a specific person experiencing a specific problem
— Curiosity Gap: Tease an outcome or reveal without giving it away
— Story Tease: Drop into the middle of a compelling story with no setup
— Contrarian Statement: Say the opposite of what the viewer expects to hear

For each hook provide:
— Spoken hook (1-2 sentences, under 20 words)
— On-screen text overlay (5 words max, high contrast, first frame)
— Opening visual direction (what the camera sees in second 1)
— Why this hook works (1 sentence on the psychological mechanism)

Rules: No "Hey guys, today I am going to..." openers. No explaining context before the hook. No slow builds. The first word must be interesting. Write for someone who is mid-scroll and has zero context about your brand.

Variables:
— Niche/Industry: [YOUR NICHE — e.g., "fitness," "personal finance," "e-commerce," "interior design"]
— Reel Topic: [WHAT THIS REEL IS ABOUT — e.g., "why most people fail at meal prepping," "how I paid off $30K in 18 months"]
— Target Audience: [WHO YOU ARE SPEAKING TO — e.g., "busy moms who want to eat healthy," "first-year freelancers," "small business owners"]
— Desired Emotion: [HOW YOU WANT THEM TO FEEL — e.g., "curious," "called out," "inspired," "validated"]

Hook 1 (Bold Claim):
Spoken: "Meal prepping is the reason most people never lose weight."
Text overlay: MEAL PREP IS BROKEN
Visual: Close-up of a perfectly organized meal-prep container being thrown in the trash
Why it works: Attacks a behavior the viewer already does, creating cognitive dissonance and forcing them to watch to understand.

Hook 2 (Direct Callout):
Spoken: "If you meal prep on Sunday and give up by Thursday, this is for you."
Text overlay: THIS IS WHY YOU QUIT
Visual: Creator pointing directly at camera, close-up face frame
Why it works: Hyper-specific scenario makes the exact viewer feel seen — they stay because this feels personal.

Hook 3 (Curiosity Gap):
Spoken: "I switched one thing in my meal prep routine and lost 11 pounds in 6 weeks."
Text overlay: THE ONE CHANGE
Visual: Side-by-side before/after food photos appearing rapidly
Why it works: States a specific, credible result and withholds the method, making leaving impossible.

Notice how each hook earns the viewer's attention through a different mechanism — one creates cognitive dissonance, one makes them feel called out, one withholds the answer. Testing these five frameworks against your audience tells you which psychological triggers your specific followers respond to most, which is data no amount of manual scripting can generate as fast.

Prompt 2: Educational/Tutorial Reel Script

Educational Reels are the highest-save format on Instagram. When someone saves your Reel, it signals to the algorithm that your content is worth keeping — and saves drive distribution almost as powerfully as shares. The key to educational Reels that get saved is specificity and actionability: the viewer must be able to apply what you teach within 24 hours. This prompt generates a complete tutorial Reel script with spoken lines, visual direction, text overlays, and a save-driving CTA structure.

Prompt IR-02: Educational/Tutorial Reel Script
Act as a short-form content strategist who specializes in educational Instagram Reels with high save rates. Write a complete tutorial Reel script following the Hook-Steps-Recap-CTA structure.

Script structure:
— Hook (0-3 sec): Bold claim or curiosity gap about the outcome the viewer will learn
— Setup (3-8 sec): One sentence establishing why this matters right now
— Steps (8-50 sec): 3-5 numbered steps, each with spoken line + on-screen text + visual action
— Recap (50-55 sec): One sentence summary of the transformation
— CTA (55-60 sec): Save-focused call to action (not just "follow me")

For each step provide:
— Step number and title (shown as text overlay)
— Spoken script (1-2 sentences max per step — tight, no filler)
— Visual direction (what appears on screen while speaking)
— Text overlay (the scannable version of the step for viewers watching on mute)

Rules: Write for viewers watching on mute (all key info must appear as text overlays). Each step must be actionable in isolation. No jargon. No padding. Total script should fit a 45-60 second Reel at natural speaking pace.

Variables:
— Niche/Industry: [YOUR NICHE]
— Tutorial Topic: [WHAT YOU ARE TEACHING — e.g., "how to write a cold email that gets replies," "3 ways to style a blazer for under $50"]
— Target Audience: [WHO WILL WATCH THIS]
— Number of Steps: [3, 4, OR 5]
— Desired Outcome for Viewer: [WHAT THEY CAN DO AFTER WATCHING — e.g., "send their first cold email," "put together 3 new outfits from existing pieces"]
— CTA Goal: [SAVE, FOLLOW, COMMENT, CLICK LINK IN BIO]

The save-focused CTA is the most underutilized element of educational Reels. Most creators end with "follow me for more tips" — a generic request that has been ignored by Instagram users for years. "Save this so you can do it this weekend" or "Save this for the next time you write a pitch" gives the viewer a specific, self-interested reason to save the content. The prompt instructs the AI to write a CTA that frames saving as useful to the viewer, not just helpful to the creator.

Prompt 3: Before-and-After Transformation Reel

Before-and-after content is the most emotionally compelling Reels format because it proves a result that the viewer either wants for themselves or wants to believe is possible. The mistake most people make with transformation Reels is jumping straight from before to after, skipping the story of how. The how is what creates connection, drives follows, and gives the viewer the context they need to believe the transformation is replicable. This prompt generates a three-part transformation Reel script: the before state, the turning point, and the after — with a framework for making the transformation feel real and attainable, not curated and aspirational.

Prompt IR-03: Before-and-After Transformation Reel
Act as a short-form storytelling coach who creates transformation content for Instagram Reels. Write a before-and-after Reel script that makes the transformation feel real, specific, and attainable — not aspirational or curated.

Script structure (30-60 seconds total):
— Before State (0-10 sec): Name the specific struggle in visceral, relatable terms. Avoid vague descriptions. Use exact details (numbers, time frames, emotions).
— Turning Point (10-25 sec): What single decision, moment, or discovery changed everything? This is the narrative engine of the Reel — make it specific and honest.
— After State (25-45 sec): Describe the result with the same specificity as the before. Include a concrete number, timeframe, or measurable outcome.
— Invitation (45-60 sec): Make the viewer feel the result is accessible to them. Tell them the first step they can take today.

For each section provide:
— Spoken script
— Visual direction (what to film for each section)
— Text overlay (key stat or phrase to display on screen)

Rules: One specific transformation per Reel. No vague outcomes. No humble bragging. The before state must be uncomfortable enough to be real. Authenticity beats production value in this format.

Variables:
— Niche: [YOUR NICHE]
— Transformation Type: [WHAT CHANGED — e.g., "revenue from $2K/month to $10K/month," "went from burned out to 4-day work week," "lost 25 lbs without a gym membership"]
— Before Details: [SPECIFIC DETAILS OF THE BEFORE STATE — numbers, feelings, daily reality]
— Turning Point: [THE MOMENT OR DECISION THAT CHANGED THINGS]
— After Details: [SPECIFIC MEASURABLE OUTCOMES]
— First Step for Viewer: [THE ONE THING THEY CAN DO TODAY TO START]

Before (0-10 sec):
Spoken: "Eighteen months ago I was making $2,200 a month freelancing and working 60-hour weeks to get there. I was charging $25 an hour because I was terrified to ask for more."
Text overlay: $2,200/MONTH. 60 HRS/WEEK.
Visual: B-roll of stressed desk setup, overflowing inbox, tired face

Turning Point (10-25 sec):
Spoken: "Then I stopped selling hours and started selling outcomes. I rewrote my entire proposal — same service, completely different framing. Instead of 'social media management, $1,500/month' I wrote: 'I will bring you 10 qualified leads a month through content, guaranteed.'"
Text overlay: SAME SERVICE. NEW FRAME.
Visual: Close-up of a proposal document being edited, then a signed contract appearing

After (25-45 sec):
Spoken: "That first outcome-based proposal landed a $4,800/month client. I now work 28 hours a week and made $11,400 last month."
Text overlay: $11,400 LAST MONTH. 28 HRS/WEEK.
Visual: Screenshot of invoice (numbers visible, client name blurred), calendar with blocked-off time

The specificity of the numbers is what makes this format work. "$11,400 last month" is more believable and more compelling than "my income tripled." The prompt explicitly requires specific numbers and timeframes in both the before and after states because specificity is what turns a transformation story from a humble brag into credible proof.

Prompt 4: Day-in-the-Life Reel Script

Day-in-the-life Reels are one of the highest-follow formats on Instagram because they let viewers step into a lifestyle, identity, or routine they aspire to. Done well, they feel like a window — authentic, unfiltered, and aspirational without being unattainable. Done poorly, they feel like a highlight reel that makes the viewer feel behind. This prompt generates a day-in-the-life script with a structure that balances aspiration with authenticity, includes honest moments alongside the polished ones, and ends with an invitation that converts viewers into followers.

Prompt IR-04: Day-in-the-Life Reel Script
Act as a lifestyle content creator with a highly engaged Instagram following. Write a day-in-the-life Reel script that feels authentic, specific, and aspirational without feeling out of reach. The goal is to attract new followers who want to live a version of this life or build a version of this business.

Script structure (45-75 seconds):
— Morning Anchor (0-8 sec): One specific, visual morning habit that signals the lifestyle or identity this Reel represents
— Scene 1 — Work/Craft (8-20 sec): A genuine behind-the-scenes glimpse of how you do the core thing you do, with one honest detail (what went wrong, what surprised you)
— Scene 2 — Break/Life (20-35 sec): A non-work moment that humanizes the day and shows what the lifestyle actually feels like
— Scene 3 — Result/Win (35-55 sec): A specific, concrete win from the day (a client response, a sale, a finished project, a personal milestone)
— Honest Reflection (55-65 sec): One thing that was hard today. This is what makes the Reel feel real and drives the follow.
— Follow CTA (65-75 sec): A specific reason to follow that is tied to what the viewer just watched

Rules: Include at least one specific number. Include one moment that did not go perfectly. No "rise and grind" energy. Write in first person, present tense, conversational voice. Total Reel should feel like watching a friend's day, not a brand advertisement.

Variables:
— Your Identity/Role: [WHO YOU ARE — e.g., "freelance copywriter," "e-commerce founder," "fitness coach," "remote software developer"]
— Lifestyle Element to Highlight: [WHAT MAKES YOUR DAY ASPIRATIONAL — e.g., "work from anywhere," "build a business around your health," "creative freedom"]
— One Honest Difficulty: [SOMETHING REAL THAT IS HARD ABOUT YOUR LIFE OR WORK]
— Today's Win: [SPECIFIC RESULT OR MILESTONE FROM TODAY]
— Reason to Follow: [WHAT YOU POST REGULARLY THAT WOULD HELP SOMEONE WHO JUST WATCHED THIS]

The honest difficulty is the most important structural element in day-in-the-life Reels. Creators who show only the wins lose credibility fast. Viewers know a perfect day is a curated day. When you include a moment that was hard — a deal that fell through, a creative block, a morning that did not go as planned — it signals that you are showing the real version, and that is what makes the aspirational moments feel earned and believable.

Prompt 5: Product Demo/Unboxing Reel

Product Reels are the highest-converting content format for e-commerce brands on Instagram. When done right, they do not look like ads — they look like a friend showing you something they just discovered and love. The key elements are: a hook that is about the problem the product solves (not about the product itself), a demo that shows the product working in a real-life context (not on a white background), and a CTA that removes friction from the purchase decision. This prompt generates a product demo or unboxing script that sells through demonstration, not description.

Prompt IR-05: Product Demo/Unboxing Reel Script
Act as a short-form commerce content creator who produces product Reels that convert viewers to buyers without looking like ads. Write a product demo or unboxing Reel script that sells through demonstration and genuine enthusiasm, not traditional advertising language.

Script structure (30-60 seconds):
— Problem Hook (0-5 sec): Open with the problem the product solves, not the product name. The viewer should feel the frustration before they see the solution.
— Reveal (5-12 sec): Introduce the product with specificity — what it is, who made it, what makes it different. Show it in hand.
— Demo Moment (12-40 sec): Show the product working in a real-life scenario. Narrate what you notice. Include one "I did not expect this" observation — unexpected specifics build trust.
— Result (40-50 sec): State the specific outcome the product created (time saved, money saved, problem solved, feeling achieved).
— Purchase CTA (50-60 sec): Remove a common objection to buying and direct to action (link in bio, story link, or swipe up).

Rules: Never open with the product name. Never use "game changer," "amazing," or "obsessed." Use specific sensory details (what it looks like, feels like, smells like, sounds like). Film in real environments, not studio. One product per Reel. Enthusiasm must feel earned by the demo, not assumed from the start.

Variables:
— Product Name: [PRODUCT NAME]
— Product Category: [CATEGORY — e.g., "skincare," "kitchen tool," "fitness equipment," "stationery"]
— Problem It Solves: [THE PAIN POINT — e.g., "dry skin that no moisturizer fixes," "meal prep taking 3 hours on Sundays," "cables everywhere on my desk"]
— Key Differentiator: [WHAT MAKES THIS PRODUCT UNIQUE]
— Price: [PRICE POINT]
— Where to Buy: [LINK IN BIO, SHOP NOW, OR SPECIFIC PLATFORM]
— Common Purchase Objection: [WHY SOMEONE MIGHT NOT BUY — e.g., "too expensive," "not sure my skin type," "already have something similar"]

The "I did not expect this" observation is one of the highest-performing structural elements in product content. When you share a specific, unexpected detail about a product — a texture you did not anticipate, a feature that is not in the product description, a side effect you noticed after a week — it signals genuine firsthand experience rather than scripted promotion. That signal is worth more to the viewer's trust than any number of positive adjectives.

Prompt 6: Trending Audio Script Adapter

Using trending audio is one of the most reliable shortcuts to expanded Reels reach. When Instagram's algorithm detects that a sound is trending, it actively boosts Reels using that audio to broader audiences. But most creators who try to use trending audio make the same mistake: they use the audio and hope the content will somehow fit. The Reels that go viral with trending audio are the ones where the content and the audio feel made for each other — where the audio amplifies the message rather than sitting underneath it as background noise. This prompt generates a Reels script adapted to work specifically with a trending sound or audio clip you have identified.

Prompt IR-06: Trending Audio Script Adapter
Act as a viral content strategist who specializes in adapting brand messaging to trending Instagram audio. Write a Reels script that uses a specific trending audio clip to amplify the message rather than just as background music.

Audio-first scripting approach:
— Identify the audio's emotional or narrative structure (what does the audio make you feel? What story arc does it create?)
— Map the brand message onto that emotional arc so the content and audio feel intentional together
— Write visuals and on-screen text that sync to the audio's beats, drops, or lyrical moments
— Write any spoken script that complements (not competes with) the audio

Provide:
— Audio analysis: What does this audio communicate emotionally and narratively?
— Content concept: How does your topic map onto this audio's arc?
— Shot-by-shot visual script: What appears on screen at each audio beat or moment
— Text overlay script: What words appear on screen and when
— Spoken script (if any): Words spoken over or between audio breaks
— Hook thumbnail: What single frame makes someone tap on this Reel in the feed

Rules: The audio must feel intentional, not incidental. Content should make sense even without the audio (since ~60% of Instagram users watch on mute). If the audio has a lyrical drop or emotional peak, the most compelling visual or text must land at that exact moment.

Variables:
— Trending Audio: [DESCRIBE THE AUDIO — e.g., "the 'oh no' sound by Kreepa," "a fast-paced piano build-up with a dramatic drop," "a voice clip saying 'this you?'"]
— Your Niche: [YOUR NICHE OR INDUSTRY]
— Content Angle: [WHAT YOU WANT TO COMMUNICATE — e.g., "the wrong way most people approach budgeting," "before vs. after my skincare routine," "things I stopped doing that changed my business"]
— Target Audience: [WHO SHOULD SEE THIS]

The synchronization of content and audio is what separates Reels that ride a trend from Reels that go viral within a trend. When the most impactful word or image lands at the audio's emotional peak — a lyrical drop, a beat change, a spoken punchline — the viewer experiences both the audio and the content as one unified thing. That synchronization is what triggers the share: people share Reels that give them a feeling, and audio-visual synchronization is one of the fastest ways to create one.

Prompt 7: Behind-the-Scenes Reel Script

Behind-the-scenes content consistently outperforms polished promotional content in engagement rate because it satisfies a fundamental human curiosity: how does this actually work? Viewers who follow a brand or creator want to see the reality behind the output they admire. They want to see the messy draft before the polished post, the failed batch before the product launch, the unglamorous process behind the aspirational result. This prompt generates a behind-the-scenes Reel script that reveals something genuinely interesting about how you work, create, or operate — without giving away competitive advantages or sensitive information.

Prompt IR-07: Behind-the-Scenes Reel Script
Act as a content strategist specializing in behind-the-scenes brand content that builds authentic connection and increases follower loyalty. Write a behind-the-scenes Reel script that gives viewers a genuine, interesting glimpse into the real process behind the result they see.

Script structure (30-60 seconds):
— Curiosity Hook (0-5 sec): Tease what they are about to see with a question or reveal they could not predict
— The Reality (5-30 sec): Show the unglamorous, specific, honest version of the process. Include one thing that is harder, stranger, or more interesting than people expect.
— The Why It Matters (30-45 sec): Connect the process to the result the viewer cares about. Why does this specific effort create the outcome they value?
— The Payoff (45-55 sec): The finished result, final product, or completed moment that the behind-the-scenes process created
— CTA (55-60 sec): Invite them behind the curtain regularly (follow prompt tied to behind-the-scenes content)

Rules: Show something genuinely surprising or counterintuitive about your process. Avoid "meet the team" blandness. One specific process per Reel. Real environments, natural lighting, and imperfect moments are better than produced studio shots. The process must be interesting on its own — not just a vehicle for product promotion.

Variables:
— What You Are Showing: [PROCESS, MOMENT, OR CREATION TO REVEAL — e.g., "how I write every newsletter in 90 minutes," "the testing process behind our candle scents," "what my content planning Sunday actually looks like"]
— Your Industry/Brand: [YOUR BUSINESS OR CREATOR NICHE]
— Surprising Element: [THE THING ABOUT THIS PROCESS THAT WOULD SURPRISE MOST PEOPLE]
— The Result Viewers Care About: [WHAT THIS PROCESS ULTIMATELY CREATES FOR YOUR AUDIENCE]
— Target Audience: [WHO IS WATCHING]

The most common behind-the-scenes mistake is making it too polished. If your behind-the-scenes Reel looks like a brand video, it defeats the purpose. Viewers are there for the imperfection — the mistake batch, the cold coffee, the fifteenth take. The prompt specifically instructs the AI to include one element that is harder, stranger, or more interesting than people expect because that unexpected detail is what transforms a behind-the-scenes clip from content into connection.

Prompt 8: Customer Testimonial Reel Script

User-generated content and customer testimonials are the most credible content on Instagram because the viewer perceives a real customer as a neutral party with no incentive to exaggerate. But a customer saying "I love this product, it is great" is not a testimonial that converts — it is a hollow endorsement that everyone ignores. A testimonial that converts is one that describes a specific problem, a specific result, and a specific moment when the viewer thought "this actually worked." This prompt generates a customer testimonial Reel script — either for the customer to read directly or for you to narrate on their behalf with their permission.

Prompt IR-08: Customer Testimonial Reel Script
Act as a conversion copywriter specializing in customer story content for Instagram Reels. Write a testimonial Reel script that feels like a genuine customer story rather than a paid endorsement. Use the customer's raw feedback as source material and shape it into a narrative that converts skeptical viewers.

Script structure (30-45 seconds):
— Problem Statement (0-8 sec): The customer names the specific problem they had before your product/service. In their words, not marketing language. As specific as possible.
— Skepticism Moment (8-18 sec): The moment they almost did not try it, or the thing they were worried about. This builds relatability for skeptical viewers.
— The Result (18-32 sec): The specific, measurable outcome. Numbers wherever possible. Time frame of when they noticed results.
— The Recommendation (32-40 sec): A natural, specific recommendation — not "you should try it" but "if you are someone who [specific situation], then this is for you."
— Brand CTA (40-45 sec): Product name, where to get it, and one friction-reducing statement (free shipping, easy returns, try it risk-free)

Rules: No superlatives without specifics. No "changed my life" without explaining how. The skepticism moment is mandatory — it is what makes skeptical viewers trust the testimonial. Write in first person from the customer's perspective. If writing for the brand to narrate, shift to third person but keep all the specifics.

Variables:
— Customer's Raw Feedback: [PASTE ACTUAL REVIEW, DM, OR COMMENT FROM CUSTOMER — even rough notes work]
— Your Product/Service: [WHAT THEY USED]
— Customer Profile: [WHO IS THIS CUSTOMER — e.g., "32-year-old mom of two," "freelance graphic designer," "first-time home cook"]
— Specific Result: [MEASURABLE OUTCOME — e.g., "lost 14 lbs in 8 weeks," "saved 5 hours a week," "first $5K month"]
— Format: [CUSTOMER ON CAMERA, BRAND NARRATING WITH B-ROLL, OR CUSTOMER VOICE NOTE OVER VISUALS]

The skepticism moment is the structural element that most testimonial scripts skip entirely — and it is the one that matters most. When a customer says "I almost did not try it because I thought it would be too complicated for someone my age," or "I had bought three other products that did not work," every skeptical viewer in the audience thinks: that is me. That recognition is what makes the subsequent result feel believable rather than convenient.

Prompt 9: Carousel-to-Reel Repurpose Script

If you have been creating carousels on Instagram — those swipe-through educational posts that perform well in the feed — you are sitting on a library of Reels content you have not made yet. Every carousel can be converted into a Reel: the headline slide becomes the hook, the numbered slides become the tutorial steps, and the call-to-action slide becomes the closing CTA. The conversion is not automatic, though. Carousel copy is written to be read, not spoken. This prompt takes your existing carousel content and rewrites it as a Reels script — adapting the pacing, visuals, and spoken language for a video format while preserving the educational value that made the carousel perform in the first place.

Prompt IR-09: Carousel-to-Reel Repurpose Script
Act as a content repurposing strategist who converts high-performing Instagram carousels into short-form Reels without losing the educational value. Take the carousel content below and rewrite it as a complete Reels script optimized for video format.

Conversion rules:
— Carousel headlines become spoken hooks — rewrite them as verbal statements, not text to be read
— Numbered slides become spoken steps — condense each slide to one sentence maximum
— Body copy becomes visual direction — what should appear on screen while speaking each point
— CTA slide becomes a verbal and visual call-to-action — include both spoken and text overlay versions

Reformat for video:
— Shorten all sentences to under 15 words (spoken sentences must be comfortable at natural pace)
— Eliminate any text that only works when read (parentheticals, em-dashes, complex punctuation)
— Add transition direction between each point (cut, zoom, text wipe, B-roll insert)
— Add text overlays for muted viewers that match the spoken script
— Estimate total runtime at natural speaking pace

Provide the full script in this format for each section:
[SECTION] | [SPOKEN SCRIPT] | [VISUAL DIRECTION] | [TEXT OVERLAY]

Variables:
— Carousel Content: [PASTE YOUR CAROUSEL COPY HERE — headline slide, all numbered slides, CTA slide]
— Original Carousel Topic: [WHAT THE CAROUSEL WAS ABOUT]
— Target Reel Length: [30 SECONDS, 45 SECONDS, OR 60 SECONDS]
— Tone Adjustment: [MORE CASUAL THAN CAROUSEL / SAME TONE / MORE ENERGETIC — specify if the spoken style should differ from the written style]

Repurposing carousels into Reels is one of the highest-leverage content activities available to creators and brands because the content strategy is already proven. You know the carousel performed. You know the audience responded to the topic. Tips for Using AI to Create Reels That Get Views

The 10 prompts above give you the scripts. These five tips ensure you film, edit, and publish them in a way that the algorithm — and your audience — rewards.

1. Write the Hook Last, Film It First

The counterintuitive truth about Reels production is that you should plan the hook last but film it first. Write the body of your Reel — the actual content — and then determine the hook based on what is most interesting about it. Once you know the hook, film it before everything else while your energy is highest and the line is freshest. Hooks filmed at the end of a session, after the creator has already said the same thing 12 times in different ways, almost always feel flat. Hooks filmed first, when you are still excited about the topic, land harder.

2. Optimize the First Frame, Not Just the Hook Line

The first frame of your Reel is the thumbnail that appears in the feed when someone has not pressed play yet. Most creators focus entirely on their spoken hook and neglect the visual hook — the image a viewer sees for half a second before deciding whether to tap. A face with a strong, readable expression, text overlay that creates immediate curiosity, or a compelling visual contrast will get the tap that gives your hook a chance to land. Before you finalize a Reel, screenshot the first frame and ask yourself: if this were a static image in my feed, would I stop and watch?

3. Caption for Muted Viewers First

Approximately 60% of Instagram users scroll without sound. That means every Reel you publish needs to communicate its value to a viewer who cannot hear you. Text overlays are not an afterthought — they are the primary content delivery mechanism for the majority of your audience. The prompts in this article include text overlay directions for every section of every script. Use them. A Reel that works perfectly on mute is a Reel that works perfectly for 60% of the audience.

4. Post Within the First Hour of Peak Engagement

Instagram Reels get their initial distribution window in the first 30–60 minutes after posting. The algorithm assesses early engagement signals — likes, comments, shares, saves, and watch time — and uses those signals to determine how broadly to distribute the Reel beyond your existing followers. Posting when your audience is most active maximizes the signals in that first hour. Check your Instagram Insights for your specific peak engagement time, and schedule your Reels to post within that window rather than posting whenever the Reel is finished.

5. Treat Every Comment as a Script Prompt

The comments section of a high-performing Reel is the best source of future Reels ideas because it shows exactly what questions, objections, and curiosities your actual audience has. When someone asks "but what about..." or "does this work for..." in the comments, that question is a hook for your next Reel. When multiple people ask the same question, that is your next tutorial topic. The creators who grow fastest on Instagram are the ones who treat comments as a content brief, not just a metric. Run those questions through the educational tutorial or hook script prompts in this article and you will never run out of content that your specific audience actually wants.

How long should an Instagram Reels script be? +
The ideal Reels length depends on your content type, but the data consistently shows that 7-15 second Reels get the highest reach (the algorithm pushes short, rewatchable content), while 30-60 second Reels work best for educational and tutorial content that delivers genuine value. Reels up to 90 seconds are supported, but completion rate drops sharply after 60 seconds for most niches. When scripting, aim for one clear idea per Reel. The scripts in this article are calibrated by format: hook scripts are designed for under 15 seconds, tutorial scripts for 30-60 seconds, and content calendar Reels are planned by day with appropriate lengths.
What makes an Instagram Reels hook effective? +
An effective Reels hook does one of three things in the first 1-3 seconds: it creates a pattern interrupt (something visually or verbally unexpected), it makes a bold or counterintuitive claim that triggers curiosity, or it directly calls out a specific pain point the viewer has right now. The worst hooks are generic openers like "Hey guys, today I want to talk about..." — they telegraph nothing and give the viewer no reason to stay. The hook prompts in this article use five proven frameworks: bold claim, question, counterintuitive statement, story tease, and direct callout. Each framework has a different psychological mechanism for stopping the scroll.
Do these AI prompts work for product-based businesses, not just creators? +
Yes. The prompts in this article are written with variables that accommodate both personal brands and product-based businesses. How do I use AI-generated Reels scripts without sounding robotic? +
The key is to use the AI script as a structural framework, not a word-for-word teleprompter. Read the AI output, identify the hook, the main points, and the CTA, then deliver each section in your own words and speaking style.
