10 AI Prompts for Home Services Marketing (HVAC, Plumbing, Roofing)

ContentKit AI•March 4, 2026•12 min read

AI prompts for home services marketing are structured instructions that generate high-converting copy for HVAC companies, plumbers, roofers, electricians, and other contractors — covering Google Ads, local SEO service pages, review requests, social media, emergency landing pages, and referral programs in minutes instead of hours. This guide delivers 10 copy-paste prompts built specifically for the home services industry, each loaded with the urgency, trust signals, and local proof that make contractors win in their market.

Home services is one of the most competitive local marketing environments on the internet. When a homeowner's air conditioner dies at 2 p.m. on a July afternoon, they search "AC repair near me," scan the top three results, and call the business that looks most trustworthy in about thirty seconds. The margin between winning that call and losing it is not the quality of your work — it is the quality of your marketing copy. Your Google ad headline, your Local Services profile, your review count, your service page.

The problem most contractors face is that great marketing copy takes time they do not have. A plumber running three crews and answering dispatch calls does not have two hours to write a service page that ranks on Google. An HVAC company owner does not have time to craft the perfect Facebook post after a big installation job. Marketing falls to the bottom of the priority list, and the contractor with the best website — not the best service — gets the lead.

This is precisely where structured ai prompts for home services marketing change the equation. Instead of staring at a blank page, you paste a prompt into ChatGPT or Claude, fill in your business details, and get a complete Google search ad, service page draft, or seasonal email in under three minutes. The AI handles the writing. You handle the work.

The 10 prompts below cover every critical marketing channel for home service contractors: Google Local Services Ads, paid search, local SEO service pages, seasonal promotions, review requests, Google Business Profile posts, social media project showcases, emergency service landing pages, referral programs, and monthly content calendars. Each prompt includes the specific variables, format guidelines, and copywriting frameworks that make the output usable — not just a generic starting point you have to rewrite entirely.

Why Home Services Marketing Requires Specific Prompts

Generic AI prompts produce generic copy. Ask an AI to "write a Google ad for my plumbing company" and you will get something safe, bland, and indistinguishable from the 47 other plumbing ads running in your city. Ask it to "write a Google Local Services Ad for a licensed master plumber in Austin serving Westlake, Tarrytown, and South Congress, with same-day service, a 4.9-star rating from 312 reviews, and a free camera inspection offer" — and you get copy that wins.

Home services marketing has specific requirements that differ from other industries. Every prompt in this article is built around these requirements.

Urgency Is Not Optional

Most home services jobs are triggered by a problem, not a planned purchase. A burst pipe, a failed furnace, a roof leak after a storm. When the trigger is urgent, the homeowner is not comparing prices carefully — they need someone now. Your marketing copy must signal availability and speed: same-day service, 24/7 emergency response, two-hour response windows. Generic copy that says "quality service at fair prices" loses to copy that says "same-day AC repair — available now."

Trust Is the Conversion Variable

A homeowner is letting a stranger into their house. The purchase decision is fundamentally a trust decision. Licensing, insurance, Google reviews, years in business, brand-name equipment partnerships, Better Business Bureau accreditation — these are not nice-to-haves in home services marketing. They are conversion variables. Every prompt in this article includes a trust signal slot because trust copy is not optional in this industry.

Local Specificity Wins

Home services is inherently local. "Plumber near me" and "HVAC repair in Phoenix" are different from "accounting software" or "online fitness program." The homeowner wants someone in their city, ideally their neighborhood. Marketing copy that names specific neighborhoods, service areas, or local landmarks outperforms generic "serving the greater metro area" language. Every prompt below includes a location variable that should be filled in with specific city names and neighborhood references, not just a zip code.

Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) appear above standard search ads for queries like "plumber near me," "HVAC repair Austin," and "roofer in Phoenix." They display a Google Guarantee or Google Screened badge, your business name, star rating, review count, hours, and a brief profile description. When four contractors appear side by side, the profile copy, review count, and photo determine who gets the call. This prompt generates optimized LSA profile copy — the business description, five highlight bullets, and top service descriptions — that wins the comparison scan.

— Review Response Template (75 words max): A template for responding to 5-star reviews that includes the trade, city, and a service-specific reference. Review responses are public and indexed by Google.

Principle: Homeowners compare 3–4 contractors in 30 seconds. Specificity wins over generic claims. "Licensed master plumber since 2009, serving South Austin same-day" beats "quality plumbing service at fair prices" every time.

"Same-Day AC Repair" or "AC Repair — Open Now" in a headline that Google serves to a searcher at 4 p.m. on a Friday converts at a significantly higher rate than a benefit headline. Always include availability signals in your home services search ads.

Prompt 5: Customer Review Request

Online reviews are the single biggest conversion driver for home services businesses. A contractor with 4.9 stars and 300 reviews wins leads over a competitor with 4.6 stars and 40 reviews, even if the lower-rated contractor does better work. The problem is that most contractors provide excellent service but never ask for a review — or they ask awkwardly at the wrong moment. This prompt generates a text message and email review request that gets a 25–40% response rate when sent within two hours of job completion.

Prompt HS-05: Customer Review Request
Act as a home services marketing specialist focused on reputation management. Write a review request message that gets homeowners to leave a 5-star Google review within 24 hours of service completion.

Provide:
— SMS version (160 characters max): Short, personal, direct. Must include the technician's name or business name, a reference to the specific service, and the Google review link placeholder.
— Email version (subject line + 100–130 word body): Warm, personal tone. Not corporate. Reference the service performed, make the ask feel easy, and include a prominent CTA to the Google review link.
— Follow-up SMS (for non-responders after 48 hours, 160 characters max): Gentle reminder, different angle.

Rules: Do not beg. Do not offer an incentive (Google's Terms of Service prohibit review gating and incentivizing reviews). Keep it personal and specific. The more the message references what actually happened on the job, the higher the response rate. Homeowners review contractors because they feel genuinely helped — remind them of that feeling.

Variables:
— Business Name: [BUSINESS NAME]
— Technician Name: [TECH'S FIRST NAME]
— Service Performed: [WHAT WAS DONE — e.g., "AC repair," "water heater replacement," "drain cleaning"]
— Customer First Name Placeholder: [FIRST NAME]
— Google Review Link: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]
— City: [CITY]

SMS (160 chars max):
Hi [Name], it's Marcus from AquaFix Plumbing. Hope the drain repair is holding up! If we did a great job, a quick Google review means the world to us: [LINK] Thank you!

Email Subject: How did your drain repair go, [Name]?

Email Body:
Hi [Name],

Marcus from AquaFix here. We finished your drain cleaning earlier today and wanted to make sure everything is running smoothly.

If our team took good care of you, we would really appreciate a quick Google review. It takes about 60 seconds and helps Austin homeowners find us when they need help fast.

Follow-up SMS:
Hi [Name], Marcus from AquaFix again. Still hoping to hear how the drain repair went. Your Google review helps other Austin homeowners: [LINK] Thanks!

Timing is the most important variable in review request performance. Messages sent within two hours of job completion generate three to four times the response rate of messages sent the next day. The service is fresh, the homeowner is relieved, and the goodwill is at its peak. Build the review request into your technician checkout process so it happens automatically at job completion, not as an afterthought.

5 Tips for Using AI Prompts in Home Services Marketing

The 10 prompts above give you the raw copy. These five tips ensure you use it effectively and avoid the mistakes that undermine AI-generated home services content.

1. Fill In Every Variable With Specifics

The most common mistake with AI marketing prompts is leaving variables vague. "City: Phoenix" produces different (and better) results than just "Phoenix" when you specify "Phoenix, serving Scottsdale, Tempe, and Chandler." "Trust Signal: licensed and insured" is weaker than "licensed master electrician since 2007, state license #EC-00045932, $2M liability insurance." The more specific the input, the more specific — and credible — the output. Never generalize a variable you could fill in with a real fact about your business.

2. Always Add Your Actual Review Count

Home services marketing lives and dies on social proof. When you fill in the review count variable with your real number — "4.8 stars, 94 reviews" — the AI outputs copy that uses that specific number throughout. Specific review counts are more persuasive than vague claims ("hundreds of happy customers") and more trustworthy than round numbers ("100+ reviews"). Update this variable every time you run a prompt so your copy always reflects your current standing.

3. Edit for Trade-Specific Voice

AI-generated copy is a draft, not a final product. Read the output aloud and ask: "Does this sound like a plumber talking to a homeowner, or does it sound like a marketing brochure?" If the answer is the latter, edit for tone. Home services marketing works best when it sounds like a skilled tradesperson who is proud of their work and confident in their expertise. Remove corporate filler phrases ("we are committed to excellence"), replace passive constructions with active ones, and add the kind of specific detail — brand names, process steps, time estimates — that a real contractor would mention.

4. A/B Test Your Ads Monthly

Do not replace a running Google search ad with an AI-generated version without testing. Create a duplicate ad group with the AI-generated copy and run both versions simultaneously with a 50/50 traffic split. After two to four weeks, compare click-through rate, conversion rate, and cost per lead. The AI-generated version will often outperform the original because it produces more variation across headline positions — but verify this with data before retiring your proven copy. Regenerate new prompt outputs monthly to keep creative fresh and catch seasonal angles your current ads are missing.

5. Localize Every Piece of Content

Generic home services copy loses to localized copy in local search and on social media. Every AI output from these prompts should include at least two to three specific neighborhood names, a reference to a local weather pattern or seasonal condition relevant to your market, and where possible, a local reference point (a neighborhood, a highway corridor, a local landmark area) that signals to the reader and to Google that you are genuinely rooted in this community. Homeowners trust local contractors. Local copy creates local trust.
