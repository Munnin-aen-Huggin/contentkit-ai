12 AI Prompts for Instagram Ads (Stories, Reels, Feed)

ContentKit AI•14 min read

AI prompts for Instagram ads are structured instructions that generate scroll-stopping captions, Reels scripts, Stories overlays, Carousel copy, and full creative briefs for every Instagram ad format — ready to paste into Meta Ads Manager. This guide delivers 12 copy-paste prompts covering Feed, Stories, Reels, Carousel, Collection, Explore, Shopping, Lead Form, Brand Awareness, A/B testing, creative briefs, and full campaign strategy.

Instagram is where over two billion people go to discover products, follow creators, and make purchase decisions every month. Unlike Google Search, where users are already looking for what you sell, Instagram advertising works by interrupting a scroll — which means your ad must earn attention in the first two seconds or disappear forever. That tension between interruption and engagement is what makes Instagram advertising simultaneously the most creative and the most technical ad platform available to marketers.

The platform's ad formats span an enormous range: a square Feed image with a 125-character primary text, a full-screen vertical Stories ad with a swipe-up link, a 15-second Reels video with an audio hook, a 10-slide Carousel walking a prospect through a product line, a shoppable Collection ad linking directly to a product catalog, a lead form ad that captures emails without leaving the app. Each format demands different copy, different creative direction, and a different understanding of where the viewer is in their scroll and what will stop them.

This is precisely where ai prompts for instagram ads change the equation. Instead of writing each format from scratch and hoping you've captured the right tone, you use a structured prompt that encodes the format's specs, the copywriting framework best suited to it, and the specific variables for your business. The AI generates format-correct, on-brand copy in under two minutes. You spend your time on strategy and testing, not on drafting.

The 12 prompts below cover every major Instagram ad format. Each prompt includes format specifications, proven creative frameworks, and variables you simply fill in. Why Instagram Ads Require Format-Specific Copy

One of the most common Instagram ad mistakes is writing one piece of copy and running it across all placements. A caption written for the Feed will feel out of place in Stories. A Stories overlay script is too abrupt for a Carousel. A Reels hook that works for video makes no sense on a static image. Instagram's placement algorithm serves different formats to different users in different mindsets — and the copy must match.

Here is why format-specific copy matters for each major placement.

Feed Ads: Discovery with Depth

Instagram Feed ads appear between organic posts as users scroll their main feed. The viewer is in a browse mindset — they are looking at content from accounts they follow and are receptive to discovery. Feed ads can run long captions (up to 2,200 characters, though the first 125 show before the "more" tap), making them the best Instagram format for nuanced storytelling, social proof stacks, and multi-benefit value propositions. A Feed ad that works is one that would look at home alongside the organic content surrounding it.

Stories Ads: Urgency and Swipe

Instagram Stories ads are full-screen, vertical, and ephemeral. The viewer is in a rapid-tap mindset — swiping through 10, 15, 20 Stories in quick succession. Your ad has approximately 3 seconds before they tap forward. Stories copy must be brutally concise: a one-line hook as text overlay, a supporting visual, and a clear CTA that links to action via swipe-up or link sticker. Long captions do not belong in Stories. Short, punchy, visual-first copy does.

Reels Ads: Native Entertainment

Reels ads are the fastest-growing Instagram placement and currently deliver the lowest average CPM. They appear within the Reels feed as users swipe through short videos, and they must feel native to that environment. A Reels ad that looks like an ad gets skipped. A Reels ad that looks like an entertaining or educational short video gets watched, shared, and clicked. The script structure matters: hook in the first 2 seconds, deliver value or story in the middle, CTA at the end. The AI prompts below encode this structure explicitly.

The bottom line: ai prompts for instagram ads are not one-size-fits-all. The 12 prompts in this article are each calibrated to the specific format they serve — format specs, copy length, creative framework, and CTA type — so you are never adapting generic copy to fit a placement it was not written for.

Prompt 1: Instagram Feed Ad (Single Image)

The Instagram Feed single image ad is the most versatile and widely used Instagram ad format. It appears in the main feed as a square (1:1) or portrait (4:5) image with a primary text caption above the image, a headline below, and a CTA button. The primary text is the most important copy element: the first 125 characters appear without tapping "more," so those characters must hook the viewer and earn the expanded read. This prompt generates a complete Feed ad copy set optimized for the 125-character above-the-fold hook plus a full caption and headline.

Provide:

— Headline (40 characters max): Appears below the image. Reinforce the CTA or the core benefit.
— CTA Button: Recommend the best option (Shop Now, Learn More, Sign Up, Get Offer, Book Now, Contact Us, Apply Now, Download).
— Suggested Image Direction (2–3 sentences): Describe the ideal image that pairs with this copy — style, subject, color palette, text overlay if needed.

Write 3 complete variations, each testing a different angle:
1. Benefit-first angle: Lead with the outcome the customer achieves
2. Social proof angle: Lead with a customer result, rating, or number
3. Problem-solution angle: Lead with the pain point, solve with the product

Variables:

— Target Audience: [WHO SEES THIS AD — e.g., "women 25-40 interested in skincare," "SaaS founders," "fitness enthusiasts"]

The three-variation structure is not optional — it is the minimum for a functional Instagram Feed campaign. Meta's algorithm needs creative variation to find the best-performing angle for your specific audience. Running one ad is a wager. Running three variations with different lead angles is a structured experiment that gives the algorithm something to optimize.

Prompt 2: Instagram Stories Ad (Vertical, Swipe-Up)

Instagram Stories ads are full-screen vertical (9:16) placements that appear between organic Stories. The viewer is in a rapid-swipe mindset, tapping forward every 3–5 seconds. Stories ads that survive that tap pattern have one thing in common: they communicate value in the first frame. This prompt generates Stories ad copy with a text overlay hook, a visual concept, and a swipe-up CTA — all calibrated to the 3-second attention window of the Stories format.

But the creative bar is the highest: Reels viewers are watching entertaining, educational, or authentic short videos, and they will swipe past anything that feels like an ad. The best Reels ads look like organic Reels. This prompt generates a full 15–30 second Reels script with hook, body, and CTA — structured to feel native to the Reels feed while driving measurable conversions.

Prompt IG-03: Instagram Reels Ad (15–30 Sec Script)
Act as an Instagram Reels content strategist and direct-response copywriter. Write a 15–30 second Reels ad script that feels like organic content but drives a specific conversion. The script must compete with entertainment for viewer attention — not with other ads.

Script structure:

— Solution or Story (8–22 sec): Introduce the product naturally. Show, do not tell. Use a demo, transformation, or "I used to do X, now I do Y" structure.
— CTA (22–30 sec): Direct, specific action. Tell the viewer exactly what to do and why to do it now.

Provide:
— Full script with speaker notes and action cues (what appears on screen)
— Suggested on-screen text overlays (for silent viewers)
— 3 alternative hook options to A/B test
— Ideal creator profile: What type of person should deliver this script?

Variables:
— Business Name: [BUSINESS NAME]
— Product/Service: [WHAT YOU SELL]
— Target Audience: [SPECIFIC VIEWER PERSONA]
— Core Problem Solved: [THE PAIN POINT YOUR PRODUCT ELIMINATES]
— Transformation: [BEFORE AND AFTER — e.g., "spending 3 hours on scheduling to scheduling in 10 minutes"]
— Social Proof: [NUMBER, RATING, OR CUSTOMER RESULT]
— Offer or CTA: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO — e.g., "link in bio," "swipe up," "first order free"]

Hook (0–2 sec): [On screen: creator holds up phone, confused expression] "Why does my Instagram take 4 hours a week to manage?"

Problem (2–8 sec): [Cut to calendar view] "Posting, scheduling, hashtags, captions — it adds up. I was spending more time managing content than creating it."

Solution (8–22 sec): [Screen recording of PostKit] "Then I found PostKit. I plan an entire week in 20 minutes. It writes captions, suggests hashtags, and schedules automatically. This is my actual workflow." [Shows 3 posts scheduled in real time]

CTA (22–30 sec): [Back to creator, direct eye contact] "First month free, link in bio. I genuinely use this every week."

On-screen overlays: "4 hours a week → 20 minutes" / "Try free, link in bio"

Alternative hooks: 1. "I stopped stressing about Instagram the day I found this." 2. "POV: Your Instagram is consistent and you didn't spend Sunday on it." 3. "The tool 12,000 creators use so they never run out of content."

The silent viewer problem is often overlooked: approximately 69% of Instagram Reels are watched without sound. Your on-screen text overlays are not decoration — they are the ad for silent viewers. Every key message in the script should have a corresponding text overlay so the video communicates fully with or without audio.

Prompt 4: Instagram Carousel Ad (Multi-Slide)

Instagram Carousel ads let you include 2–10 slides, each with its own image or video, headline, and link. They are the most powerful format for storytelling, product showcases, feature comparisons, and step-by-step education because each swipe deepens engagement. Carousel ads typically deliver 3x higher engagement rates than single-image ads because the swipe mechanic creates an interactive experience that the algorithm rewards. This prompt generates a complete 5–8 slide Carousel with a narrative arc that drives swipes and converts at the final slide.

Prompt IG-04: Instagram Carousel Ad (Multi-Slide)
Act as an Instagram Carousel ad specialist. Write a 6-slide Carousel ad that uses the swipe mechanic to build engagement and drive conversion. Each slide must earn the next swipe while building toward a final CTA slide.

— Slide 6 (CTA): Clear, specific conversion slide. Reinforce the offer and direct the viewer to action.

For each slide, provide:
— Slide Headline (40 characters max): Appears below the image in the feed
— On-image text or overlay (15 words max): The text that lives on the slide image itself
— Visual concept (1–2 sentences): What the image or design looks like

Primary Text (for the overall Carousel post, 200 characters max): Appears above slide 1. Must hook the viewer into swiping.

Choose one Carousel narrative strategy:
A. Before/after story (problem to transformation)
B. Feature showcase (one key feature per slide)
C. Step-by-step guide (numbered steps toward a result)
D. Social proof stack (one customer result per slide)

The first slide of a Carousel is everything. If it does not earn a swipe, the remaining five slides never get seen. Use a teaser structure: "5 reasons your Instagram ads are failing (swipe to see them all)" forces the swipe because the viewer cannot resolve the tension without swiping. The prompt above instructs the AI to build this open-loop tension into every first slide.

Prompt 6: Instagram Explore Ad

Explore ads do not appear in the Explore grid itself; they appear when a user taps on a post in Explore and then scrolls through the feed that follows. That means your ad appears to someone who is already in an engaged, discovery mindset — which makes it one of the highest-engagement placements available. This prompt generates Explore ad copy calibrated to cold-audience discovery intent.

This prompt generates brand awareness ad copy that builds recall and emotional association rather than driving immediate action.

Prompt 10: Instagram A/B Test Ad Variations

Running Instagram ads without a structured testing framework is guesswork. A/B testing — isolating one variable at a time and measuring which version performs better — is how top Instagram advertisers systematically discover what works and compound performance gains over time. The challenge is generating enough meaningful variations to test. Most advertisers change two words and call it a test. This prompt generates a complete A/B testing matrix: 3 hook variations, 2 caption angle variations, and 2 CTA variations — giving you a structured testing plan with enough variation to surface real insights.

Prompt IG-10: Instagram A/B Test Ad Variations
Act as a Meta Ads testing specialist. Generate a complete A/B testing matrix for an Instagram ad campaign. Each variation must test a genuinely different angle — not minor word changes, but fundamentally different creative approaches that would produce different psychological responses in the viewer.

Provide a testing matrix with:

Test 1: Hook variations (3 options for the first 1–3 seconds of the ad)
— Hook A: Pattern interrupt / surprising statistic
— Hook B: Relatable problem or pain point
— Hook C: Social proof / authority signal

Test 2: Caption angle variations (2 options for full primary text)
— Caption A: Benefit-led (what they gain)
— Caption B: Loss aversion-led (what they avoid)

Test 3: CTA variations (2 options)
— CTA A: Soft, low-commitment ask
— CTA B: Direct, urgency-driven ask

For each variation, provide the full copy, not a description of what it would say.

Testing protocol note: Run Hook A/B/C against the same caption and CTA first (hold other variables constant). After 72 hours and 1,000+ impressions, identify the winning hook. Then test Caption A vs. B using the winning hook. Then test CTA A vs. B.

Variables:
— Business Name: [BUSINESS NAME]
— Product/Service: [WHAT YOU SELL]
— Target Audience: [WHO SEES THESE ADS]

5 Tips for Using AI Prompts to Run Better Instagram Ads

The 12 prompts above give you the copy framework. These five tips ensure the copy performs in the actual campaign environment.

1. Match Copy Tone to the Placement Mindset

Every Instagram placement puts the viewer in a different mindset. Feed viewers are leisurely browsing. Stories viewers are rapidly swiping. Reels viewers are seeking entertainment. Explore viewers are seeking discovery. The same copy does not work across all four. When you use any prompt above, the first thing to do after generating copy is to read it aloud in the context of how someone experiences that placement. If Feed copy is too terse or Stories copy is too long, adjust before uploading. The prompts are calibrated by format, but your business context may require tonal adjustments the AI could not anticipate.

2. Always Generate the Hook Separately

The hook — the first 1–3 seconds of a video or the first line of a caption — determines whether the rest of your copy is ever seen. Generate 5–10 hook options using the A/B testing prompt (Prompt 10) and test them independently before scaling. A hook that improves thumb-stop rate from 20% to 40% doubles the effective reach of your entire ad budget. No other optimization delivers that kind of leverage. Treat hooks as a separate creative category and spend proportionally more time testing them.

3. Use the Creative Brief Prompt for Every Campaign

Even if you are producing creative yourself rather than briefing a designer, the creative brief prompt forces you to make decisions before you are in the production environment. What is the one message this ad must communicate? What is the exact format spec? What does the ideal viewer look like and what are they thinking when they see this? Answering these questions in writing before opening Canva or filming a Reel saves hours of revision and produces more focused creative.

4. Refresh Creative Every 2–3 Weeks

Ad fatigue is the silent campaign killer on Instagram. Unlike Google Search, where your ad appears in response to a specific query and user intent varies by session, Instagram users see the same ads repeatedly in their feed. Once an audience has seen your creative 3–5 times, CTR drops, CPM rises, and performance deteriorates. Use the prompts in this article to generate fresh variations every 2–3 weeks. The variables make refreshing fast: update the hook, change the social proof stat, adjust the offer, and you have a new creative set that feels fresh to an audience that was starting to tune you out.

5. Layer AI-Generated Copy with Real Customer Language

The strongest Instagram ad copy borrows directly from how real customers talk about your product. Pull exact phrases from your reviews, DMs, and support conversations — language like "I finally stopped wasting money on products that don't work" or "I used to spend four hours on this, now it takes twenty minutes" — and weave those verbatim phrases into the copy the AI generates. The AI produces the structure and the framework. Real customer language makes the copy feel true. That combination — proven framework plus authentic voice — consistently outperforms purely AI-generated or purely manually-written copy.

What is the best Instagram ad format for direct response? +
Reels ads consistently deliver the lowest CPM and highest reach of any Instagram placement as of 2026, making them the most efficient format for top-of-funnel awareness and direct response. For bottom-of-funnel conversions, Carousel ads with product-specific slides and Shopping ads with tagged products perform best. The right format depends on your objective: use Reels for reach, Carousels for consideration, and Shopping ads for purchases.

Instagram Reels ads perform best at 15–30 seconds. The first 1–3 seconds are critical: if the hook does not stop the scroll, the viewer swipes past. Write your script with an attention-grabbing hook in the first 2 seconds, the core message in seconds 3–20, and a clear call-to-action in the final 5 seconds.
